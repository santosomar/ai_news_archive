AI Researcher Agent Report for 2025-11-14-12-30:

The following are the insights about the papers and news:

### Summary
- [An Efficient and Almost Optimal Solver for the Joint Routing-Assignment Problem via Partial JRA and Large-{\alpha} Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09563): This paper presents a novel approach to solving the Joint Routing-Assignment (JRA) problem, achieving high accuracy and computational efficiency. The method introduces a Partial Path Reconstruction (PPR) solver and a global Large-{\alpha} constraint, demonstrating near-optimal solutions on benchmark datasets.
- [Variable Neighborhood Search for the Electric Vehicle Routing Problem](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09570): This paper discusses a winning approach from a competition focused on the Electric Vehicle Routing Problem (EVRP), utilizing Variable Neighborhood Search (VNS) to achieve superior results on the competition dataset.
- [SynthTools: A Framework for Scaling Synthetic Tools for Agent Development](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09572): SynthTools is introduced as a framework for generating synthetic tool ecosystems to aid in the development and evaluation of AI agents, achieving high reliability and scalability.
- [Proceedings of the Second International Workshop on Next-Generation Language Models for Knowledge Representation and Reasoning (NeLaMKRR 2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09575): This workshop aims to explore the reasoning capabilities of language models and their integration with logic-based knowledge representation methods.
- [Cogent argument extensions are weakly admissible but not vice versa](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09600): This research note discusses the relationship between cogent and weakly admissible argumentation frameworks, proving that cogent extensions are weakly admissible.
- [Rebellion: Noise-Robust Reasoning Training for Audio Reasoning Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09682): This paper introduces Rebellion, a robust training method for audio reasoning models to protect against advanced audio jailbreaks while maintaining performance.
- [Echoing: Identity Failures when LLM Agents Talk to Each Other](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09710): This study investigates the phenomenon of "echoing" in conversations between LLM agents, where agents mirror each other's behavior, leading to failures in achieving their intended objectives.
- [ProbLog4Fairness: A Neurosymbolic Approach to Modeling and Mitigating Bias](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09768): This paper presents a framework for modeling and mitigating algorithmic bias using ProbLog, demonstrating its effectiveness on synthetic and real-world datasets.
- [AI Annotation Orchestration: Evaluating LLM verifiers to Improve the Quality of LLM Annotations in Learning Analytics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09785): This research evaluates the effectiveness of verification-oriented orchestration in improving the quality of LLM annotations in educational contexts.
- [Why Open Small AI Models Matter for Interactive Art](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09788): This position paper argues for the importance of open small AI models in interactive art, emphasizing the control and autonomy they provide to artists.
- [SlideBot: A Multi-Agent Framework for Generating Informative, Reliable, Multi-Modal Presentations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09804): SlideBot is introduced as a multi-agent framework for generating effective presentation slides, integrating LLMs with retrieval and structured planning.
- [Robust Watermarking on Gradient Boosting Decision Trees](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09822): This paper presents a robust watermarking framework for Gradient Boosting Decision Trees, demonstrating high embedding rates and resistance to post-deployment fine-tuning.
- [Thermally Activated Dual-Modal Adversarial Clothing against AI Surveillance Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09829): This research proposes a novel adversarial wearable that uses thermochromic dyes to evade detection by AI surveillance systems.
