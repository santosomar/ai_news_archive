- [Multi-Agent Path Finding (MAPF) aims to move agents from their start to goal vertices on a graph. Lifelong MAPF (LMAPF) continuously assigns new goals to agents as they complete current ones. To guide agents' movement in LMAPF, prior works have proposed Guidance Graph Optimization (GGO) methods to optimize a guidance graph, which is a bidirected weighted graph whose directed edges represent moving and waiting actions with edge weights being action costs. Higher edge weights represent higher action costs. However, edge weights only provide soft guidance. An edge with a high weight only discourages agents from using it, instead of prohibiting agents from traversing it. In this paper, we explore the need to incorporate edge directions optimization into GGO, providing strict guidance. We generalize GGO to Mixed Guidance Graph Optimization (MGGO), presenting two MGGO methods capable of optimizing both edge weights and directions. The first optimizes edge directions and edge weights in two phases separately. The second applies Quality Diversity algorithms to optimize a neural network capable of generating edge directions and weights. We also incorporate traffic patterns relevant to edge directions into a GGO method, making it capable of generating edge-direction-aware guidance graphs.

To guide agents' movement in LMAPF, prior works have proposed Guidance Graph Optimization (GGO) methods to optimize a guidance graph, which is a bidirected weighted graph whose directed edges represent moving and waiting actions with edge weights being action costs. Higher edge weights represent higher action costs. However, edge weights only provide soft guidance. An edge with a high weight only discourages agents from using it, instead of prohibiting agents from traversing it. In this paper, we explore the need to incorporate edge directions optimization into GGO, providing strict guidance. We generalize GGO to Mixed Guidance Graph Optimization (MGGO), presenting two MGGO methods capable of optimizing both edge weights and directions. The first optimizes edge directions and edge weights in two phases separately. The second applies Quality Diversity algorithms to optimize a neural network capable of generating edge directions and weights. We also incorporate traffic patterns relevant to edge directions into a GGO method, making it capable of generating edge-direction-aware guidance graphs.

### Summary of Papers and News Related to Using AI for Security or Securing AI

#### Key Themes and Trends
1. **Security in AI Systems**: A significant focus is on enhancing the security of AI systems, particularly in the context of large language models (LLMs) and their applications. Papers discuss methods for ensuring that AI-generated content adheres to safety and ethical standards, addressing issues like hallucinations and biases.

2. **Robustness and Reliability**: Many studies emphasize the need for AI systems to be robust against adversarial attacks and to maintain performance under various conditions. Techniques such as adversarial fine-tuning and the use of uncertainty quantification are explored to improve the reliability of AI models.

3. **Federated Learning and Privacy**: Several papers highlight the importance of federated learning frameworks that allow for model training without compromising user data privacy. This is particularly relevant in sensitive domains like healthcare and finance, where data security is paramount.

4. **Explainability and Interpretability**: There is a growing emphasis on making AI systems more interpretable, especially in high-stakes applications. Techniques for providing explanations for AI decisions are being developed to enhance user trust and facilitate better decision-making.

5. **Benchmarking and Evaluation**: The introduction of new benchmarks for evaluating AI systems, particularly in the context of security and robustness, is a recurring theme. These benchmarks aim to provide standardized metrics for assessing the performance of AI models in real-world scenarios.

#### Notable Papers and Findings
- **Human Supervision as an Information Bottleneck**: This paper discusses how human-generated data can introduce biases and errors in AI systems, emphasizing the need for auxiliary signals to improve supervision capacity.

- **Learning to Generate Secure Code**: This work presents a framework for secure code generation that reduces vulnerabilities in AI-generated code, highlighting the importance of token-level rewards in reinforcement learning for code security.

- **Adversarial Fine-tuning in Offline-to-Online Reinforcement Learning**: This study introduces a method for improving the robustness of reinforcement learning policies through adversarial fine-tuning, which enhances the adaptability of AI systems in uncertain environments.

- **Trust Region Masking for Long-Horizon LLM Reinforcement Learning**: This paper proposes a method for ensuring that LLMs maintain performance during long-horizon tasks by implementing trust region constraints, which can help prevent catastrophic failures.

- **Jailbreak Foundry**: This framework aims to automate the evaluation of jailbreak techniques for LLMs, providing a systematic approach to understanding vulnerabilities in AI systems.

- **Fairness-in-the-Workflow**: This research explores how machine learning practitioners at big tech companies approach fairness in recommender systems, identifying challenges and proposing actionable recommendations.

### Insights and Implications
- **Need for Robustness**: As AI systems become more integrated into critical applications, ensuring their robustness against adversarial attacks and operational failures is crucial. This includes developing frameworks that can adapt to new threats and maintain performance.

- **Importance of Explainability**: As AI systems are deployed in sensitive areas, the ability to explain their decisions becomes increasingly important. This not only helps in building trust but also in ensuring accountability in AI applications.

- **Federated Learning as a Solution**: The use of federated learning frameworks can help address privacy concerns while still allowing for effective model training. This approach is particularly relevant in healthcare and finance, where data sensitivity is a major concern.

- **Benchmarking for Security**: The development of standardized benchmarks for evaluating the security and robustness of AI systems can facilitate better comparisons and improvements across different models and applications.

- **Collaborative Approaches**: The integration of human oversight and collaborative frameworks in AI systems can enhance their effectiveness and reliability, particularly in complex decision-making scenarios.

### Conclusion
The ongoing research and developments in AI security and robustness highlight the critical need for frameworks that ensure the safe deployment of AI technologies. As AI systems become more prevalent in various domains, addressing issues of security, explainability, and fairness will be essential for their successful integration into society.
