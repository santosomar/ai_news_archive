#### 1. **AI Models and Security Risks**
   - **Title:** *Membership Inference Attacks on LLM-based Recommender Systems*
     - **Summary:** This paper explores the vulnerabilities of LLM-based recommender systems to membership inference attacks, where attackers can determine whether specific user data was used in training. It introduces four types of attacks, revealing significant risks associated with user privacy in AI systems.

   - **Title:** *A Systematic Approach to Predict the Impact of Cybersecurity Vulnerabilities Using LLMs*
     - **Summary:** This work presents TRIAGE, a method for mapping Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVEs) to relevant techniques from the ATT&CK knowledge base using LLMs. It highlights the potential of LLMs to automate the assessment of cybersecurity vulnerabilities.

   - **Title:** *HonestCyberEval: An AI Cyber Risk Benchmark for Automated Software Exploitation*
     - **Summary:** This benchmark evaluates AI models' capabilities in automated software exploitation, focusing on their ability to detect and exploit vulnerabilities. It assesses various LLMs, revealing significant differences in performance and efficiency.

   - **Title:** *Secure Reinforcement Learning via Shuffle Privacy Model*
     - **Summary:** This paper introduces a new privacy model for reinforcement learning that enhances security without requiring a fully trusted server. It presents a novel algorithm for episodic RL under the shuffle model, demonstrating strong privacy guarantees.
