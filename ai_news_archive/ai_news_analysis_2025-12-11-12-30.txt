AI Researcher Agent Report for 2025-12-11-12-30:

The following are the insights about the papers and news:

### Summary
- [Calibrated Trust in Dealing with LLM Hallucinations: A Qualitative Study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09088): This paper investigates how hallucinations from Large Language Models (LLMs) affect user trust and interaction. It finds that hallucinations lead to context-sensitive trust calibration rather than blanket mistrust, and proposes practical recommendations for responsible LLM use.
- [AI TIPS 2.0: A Comprehensive Framework for Operationalizing AI Governance](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09114): This paper presents AI TIPS, a framework addressing governance challenges in AI deployment, emphasizing tailored risk assessments and actionable controls for specific use cases.
- [A Categorical Analysis of Large Language Models and Why LLMs Circumvent the Symbol Grounding Problem](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09117): This paper discusses how LLMs transform content into propositions and argues that they circumvent rather than solve the symbol grounding problem.
- [SDialog: A Python Toolkit for End-to-End Agent Building, User Simulation, Dialog Generation, and Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09142): SDialog is introduced as a toolkit for building and analyzing LLM-based conversational agents, integrating dialog generation, evaluation, and interpretability.
- [Visual Categorization Across Minds and Models: Cognitive Analysis of Human Labeling and Neuro-Symbolic Integration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09340): This paper contrasts human and AI strategies in visual categorization, revealing insights into cognitive processes and suggesting future neuro-symbolic architectures.
- [Architectures for Building Agentic AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09458): This chapter discusses the architectural properties that contribute to the reliability of agentic AI systems, providing design guidance for various agent types.
- [Toward Closed-loop Molecular Discovery via Language Model, Property Alignment and Strategic Search](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09566): The Trio framework is proposed for effective molecular design, integrating generative modeling and reinforcement learning to enhance drug discovery processes.
- [An End-to-end Planning Framework with Agentic LLMs and PDDL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09629): This framework utilizes LLMs for planning tasks, converting human specifications into PDDL models and generating plans without human intervention.
- [Gaussian Process Aggregation for Root-Parallel Monte Carlo Tree Search with Continuous Actions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09727): This paper introduces a method for aggregating statistics in Monte Carlo Tree Search, improving performance in environments with continuous action spaces.
- [Analyzing Planner Design Trade-offs for MAPF under Realistic Simulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09736): This work investigates how planner design choices affect performance in Multi-Agent Path Finding (MAPF) under realistic conditions.
- [RIFT: A Scalable Methodology for LLM Accelerator Fault Assessment using Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09829): RIFT is introduced as a framework for efficient fault assessment in LLM accelerators, achieving significant speedup and improved fault coverage.
- [Interpretation as Linear Transformation: A Cognitive-Geometric Model of Belief and Meaning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09831): This paper develops a geometric framework for modeling belief and meaning, exploring how beliefs propagate and mutate across cognitive agents.
- [Comparing AI Agents to Cybersecurity Professionals in Real-World Penetration Testing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.09882): This study evaluates AI agents against human cybersecurity professionals, revealing strengths and weaknesses in vulnerability discovery.
