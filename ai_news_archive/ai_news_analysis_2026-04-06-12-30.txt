### Summary of Papers and News Related to Using AI for Security or Securing AI

#### 1. **Security Vulnerabilities in AI Systems**
   - **Title: Credential Leakage in LLM Agent Skills: A Large-Scale Empirical Study**
     - This study investigates vulnerabilities in AI agent skills, revealing 520 skills with 1,708 issues, highlighting the risk of credential leakage through debug logging and emphasizing the need for better security measures in AI systems.

   - **Title: Supply-Chain Poisoning Attacks Against LLM Coding Agent Skill Ecosystems**
     - The paper introduces Document-Driven Implicit Payload Execution (DDIPE), a method to embed malicious logic in code examples, demonstrating how supply-chain attacks can hijack an agent's action space, emphasizing the need for robust security measures in AI coding agents.

   - **Title: LogicPoison: Logical Attacks on Graph Retrieval-Augmented Generation**
     - This work presents a novel attack framework that targets logical reasoning in GraphRAG systems, showing how logical connections can be corrupted to degrade performance, thus highlighting vulnerabilities in AI systems that rely on structured knowledge.

   - **Title: When AI Gets it Wrong: Reliability and Risk in AI-Assisted Medication Decision Systems**
     - The paper discusses the risks associated with AI systems in healthcare, emphasizing the potential for severe consequences from incorrect recommendations and the need for robust evaluation mechanisms.

   - **Title: A Systematic Security Evaluation of OpenClaw and Its Variants**
     - This evaluation identifies substantial security vulnerabilities across various OpenClaw agent frameworks, highlighting the risks associated with agentized systems and the need for lifecycle-wide security governance.

#### 2. **Mitigation Strategies and Frameworks**
   - **Title: Sentinel Agents Suffice for Enterprise Automation**
     - This paper argues for the effectiveness of simple terminal agents over complex systems, suggesting that straightforward programmatic interfaces can enhance security and reliability in enterprise automation.

   - **Title: SafeSci: Safety Evaluation of Large Language Models in Science Domains and Beyond**
     - SafeSci provides a framework for evaluating the safety of LLMs in scientific contexts, emphasizing the importance of understanding failure behavior and real-world impact.

   - **Title: Council Mode: Mitigating Hallucination and Bias in LLMs via Multi-Agent Consensus**
